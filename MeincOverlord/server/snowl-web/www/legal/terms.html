Game Structure

The Daily Millionaire game structure may vary from time to time at the Company's sole discretion. The "base case" game structure is a "bracket elimination" structure generally analogous to bracket structure of the annual NCAA March Madness™ tournament. But where the NCAA March Madness™ brackets pair 64 teams in head-to-head competition across 6 bracket extensions, the Daily Millionaire Game platform can pair up to 1,048,576 players in head-to-head competitions across 20 bracket extensions. (Neither the NCAA nor any of its affiliates or member schools is a sponsor of Daily Millionaire—this comparison is used for descriptive purposes only.) The actual number of players can vary based on enrollment in each game. The minimum number of players is 128 per game; the maximum is 1,048,576. Payout amounts, expected profit and similar variables will automatically adjust to the total number of game players for each game. In each game, whether with 1,048,576 players or 128 players, the structure is always the same: head-to-head competition across the allotted number of brackets: 20 brackets for 1,048,576 players, 7 brackets for 128 players, or anything in between, with each winner advancing to the next bracket, until a grand prize winner is declared. In the event an uneven number of contestants enter (i.e., a total number that is not an exponent of 2), a bracket extension is filled to the extra number of contestants that exceeds a full bracket. In this case, then, the outermost bracket would not be completely filled, but the head-to-head contests would proceed nonetheless, with the first round effectively becoming a "play-in" round for the last entrants. There is, then, an incentive to register early for the game, to avoid landing in an outer "play-in" bracket and, effectively, needing to complete the first round of competition just to arrive at the main elimination bracket
